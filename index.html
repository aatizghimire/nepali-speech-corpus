Nepali Audio Data Collection Project

Creating a diverse and inclusive Nepali speech dataset for research and development

About the Project

This project aims to create an open-source, high-quality Nepali audio dataset that can be used to advance natural language processing (NLP) and speech recognition technologies for underrepresented languages. We are working with students from diverse ethnic, regional, and linguistic backgrounds to ensure a balanced and representative dataset.

The recorded samples will be curated, anonymized, and released publicly for use in academic research, development of speech-to-text systems, and linguistic studies of the Nepali language.

Ethical Considerations

Ethical research practices are at the core of this initiative. All participants are required to give informed consent prior to recording their voice samples. We follow institutional ethical guidelines and ensure voluntary participation without coercion or compensation.

We encourage culturally sensitive content and avoid phrases or texts that might be offensive, biased, or discriminatory. Each participant has the right to withdraw from the project at any time.

Privacy Policy

Protecting participant privacy is a fundamental priority. All personal information collected (such as gender, ethnicity, and region) is used only for anonymized statistical analysis to ensure demographic coverage. No names or contact details are stored or shared.

The collected audio files will be stripped of any metadata and will not contain any personally identifiable information. The dataset will be freely accessible under a suitable open data license (e.g., CC BY-NC 4.0).

Access the Dataset

Upon completion of data collection and curation, the dataset will be published in open repositories such as GitHub, Hugging Face Datasets, and Zenodo. A subset of the data may be made available for preview with appropriate citations.

Interested students and contributors can participate by submitting audio through the Google Form. The dataset will be useful for training ASR models, dialect classification systems, and language identification tasks.
